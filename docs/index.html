Abstract

We propose MetaLens, a framework that exploits a physically-fabricated metasurface lens as an optical encryption device. The lens imposes a spatially-varying point-spread function (SV-PSF) — characterised by a ring-shaped kernel with off-axis coma-like aberrations — on every captured scene, producing an image that appears as structured noise to any observer without the key.

An authorized receiver who possesses the PSF applies patch-wise non-blind deconvolution and faithfully reconstructs the original scene. An adversary who relies on blind deblurring cannot recover meaningful content: the PSF is spatially varying, ring-shaped, and unknown, causing any spatially-uniform blind estimator to converge to a smeared, incorrect kernel.

Our long-term objective is to optimize the metasurface parameters end-to-end so as to maximize the authorized-versus-adversary performance gap, using a differentiable PSF generator jointly trained against both decoders in an adversarial loop.

The Metasurface PSF

The metasurface produces an SV-PSF stored as a \((7 \times 7 \times 101 \times 101)\) array: 49 spatially distinct kernels, each \(101 \times 101\) pixels, sampled on a uniform grid over the image field. Kernels exhibit a ring/donut structure that rotates and develops asymmetric coma-like tails toward the field corners — a hallmark of off-axis meta-optic aberrations.

This complex, position-dependent structure makes blind PSF estimation from a single observation computationally intractable. An algorithm that assumes a spatially uniform kernel will converge to a smeared average far from the true local kernel at any field position.

Method

Authorized Decoder (Non-blind)

The authorized receiver applies patch-wise Wiener deconvolution with the known SV-PSF. Each of the 49 image patches is independently deconvolved in the frequency domain:

\[ \hat{X}(\omega) = \frac{H^*(\omega)}{|H(\omega)|^2 + \lambda} \cdot Y(\omega) \]

where \(H(\omega)\) is the DFT of the local PSF kernel, \(Y(\omega)\) is the DFT of the observed patch, and \(\lambda = 5 \times 10^{-2}\) is the regularisation constant. The kernel is zero-padded and circularly shifted so that its centre lies at array position \((0,0)\) before computing the DFT, eliminating any phase offset in the recovered image. A kernel crop of \(71 \times 71\) pixels is used, retaining 96.6% of the total kernel energy.

Blind Adversary

The adversary applies the Unsupervised Wiener-Hunt algorithm (a Gibbs sampler under a Gaussian image prior) to the entire blurred image, with no knowledge of the PSF. The algorithm jointly estimates the latent image and a single spatially-uniform kernel via alternating Bayesian inference steps.

Because the true PSF is spatially varying and ring-shaped, this uniform assumption is fundamentally violated: the estimator converges to a wrong, smeared kernel, producing a recovery that is visually indistinguishable from noise. No amount of iteration mitigates this structural mismatch.

Optimization Framework Proposed

Once the differentiable metasurface PSF generator is available, we will run a two-phase adversarial training loop to find a parameter set \(\varphi\) that maximises the security gap end-to-end. The optimization objective is:

\[ \max_{\varphi} \; \mathrm{PSNR}\!\left(D_\mathrm{nb}\!\left(B_\varphi(x),\,\varphi\right),\,x\right) \;-\; \lambda \cdot \mathrm{PSNR}\!\left(D_\mathrm{b}\!\left(B_\varphi(x)\right),\,x\right) \] \[ \text{subject to} \quad \varphi \in \Phi_{\mathrm{realizable}} \]

where \(D_\mathrm{nb}\) is the authorized non-blind decoder (receives \(y\) and \(\varphi\)), \(D_\mathrm{b}\) is the blind adversary (receives \(y\) only), \(B_\varphi\) is the differentiable SV-PSF blur operator, and \(\Phi_{\mathrm{realizable}}\) is the manifold of physically realizable metasurface parameters.

Preliminary Results

We evaluate classical baselines on three standard test images, each resized to \(1008 \times 1008\) pixels (a \(7 \times 144\) pixel patch grid, ensuring each patch is substantially larger than the 71-pixel kernel crop). No training data are required: the non-blind decoder uses the exact PSF analytically. All metrics are computed against the clean original.

However, SOTA blind networks (NAFNet, Restormer) applied zero-shot close the gap to ≈0 dB, matching the authorized receiver without PSF knowledge. This motivates adversarial PSF optimization.
