Optical Encryption via
Spatially-Varying Metasurface PSF

Weiheng Tang1
1Work in progress  ·  2026

Abstract

We exploit a physically-fabricated metasurface lens as an optical encryption device. The lens imposes a spatially-varying point-spread function (SV-PSF) — characterised by a ring-shaped kernel with off-axis coma-like aberrations — on every captured scene, producing an image that appears as structured noise to any observer without the key.

An authorized receiver who possesses the PSF applies non-blind deconvolution and faithfully reconstructs the original scene. An adversary without the PSF faces a much harder blind deblurring problem: classical blind estimators (e.g., Unsupervised Wiener-Hunt) fail due to the spatially-varying, ring-shaped nature of the kernel. However, we find that state-of-the-art deep blind restoration networks (NAFNet, Restormer) applied zero-shot close the security gap to approximately zero, matching the authorized decoder without any PSF knowledge.

Our long-term objective is to optimize the metasurface parameters end-to-end so as to maximize the authorized-versus-adversary performance gap, using a differentiable PSF generator jointly trained against both decoders in an adversarial loop.

The Metasurface PSF

The metasurface produces an SV-PSF stored as a \((7 \times 7 \times 101 \times 101)\) array: 49 spatially distinct kernels, each \(101 \times 101\) pixels, sampled on a uniform grid over the image field. Kernels exhibit a ring/donut structure that rotates and develops asymmetric coma-like tails toward the field corners — a hallmark of off-axis meta-optic aberrations.

This complex, position-dependent structure makes blind PSF estimation from a single observation computationally intractable. An algorithm that assumes a spatially uniform kernel will converge to a smeared average far from the true local kernel at any field position.

Method

Wiener Deconvolution (Authorized, Non-blind)

The authorized receiver applies patch-wise Wiener deconvolution with the known SV-PSF. The full \(1008 \times 1008\) image is processed with all 49 local kernels, and the per-pixel outputs are blended with bilinear weights to avoid grid artifacts. Each kernel contribution is computed in the frequency domain:

\[ \hat{X}_{ij}(\omega) = \frac{H_{ij}^*(\omega)}{|H_{ij}(\omega)|^2 + \lambda} \cdot Y(\omega) \]

where \(H_{ij}(\omega)\) is the DFT of the \((i,j)\)-th PSF kernel, \(Y(\omega)\) is the DFT of the full blurred image, and \(\lambda = 5 \times 10^{-2}\) is the regularisation constant. Each kernel is zero-padded to image size and circularly shifted so that its centre lies at array position \((0,0)\) before the DFT, eliminating phase offset. A \(71 \times 71\) crop retaining 96.6% of total kernel energy is used.

DPIR — Deep Plug-and-Play (Authorized, Non-blind)

DPIR (Zhang et al., TPAMI 2022) casts image restoration as a Plug-and-Play Half-Quadratic Splitting (PnP-HQS) problem, replacing the hand-crafted prior with a pretrained DRUNet denoiser. Because the authorized receiver knows the full SV-PSF, we perform a single global Wiener deconvolution step on the blurred observation, then iteratively refine with DRUNet under an annealed noise schedule:

\[ z_k = \alpha_k\,\hat{X}_\text{Wiener} + (1-\alpha_k)\,x_k, \qquad x_{k+1} = \mathcal{D}_{\sigma_k}(z_k) \]

where \(\hat{X}_\text{Wiener}\) is the fixed Wiener output, \(\mathcal{D}_{\sigma_k}\) is DRUNet conditioned on noise level \(\sigma_k\), and \(\alpha_k = 1 - k/K\) linearly decays the anchor weight over \(K=8\) iterations. The noise level is annealed from \(\sigma_0 = 49/255\) to \(\sigma_{K-1} = 2.55/255\). This keeps the estimate grounded to the data-fidelity Wiener solution while progressively removing ringing artifacts.

Unsupervised Wiener-Hunt (Adversary, Blind)

The adversary applies the Unsupervised Wiener-Hunt algorithm (Pereyra et al., 2015) — a Gibbs sampler under a Gaussian image prior — to the blurred image with no knowledge of the PSF. The algorithm jointly estimates the latent image and a single spatially-uniform kernel via alternating Bayesian inference steps:

\[ p(x,h \mid y) \;\propto\; p(y \mid x,h)\,p(x)\,p(h) \]

Because the true PSF is spatially varying and ring-shaped, the uniform kernel assumption is fundamentally violated: the Gibbs sampler converges to a smeared, incorrect kernel, and no amount of iteration can mitigate this structural mismatch. This method achieves only 2–3 dB below the authorized Wiener decoder, establishing a baseline security gap.

NAFNet (Adversary, Blind Deep Network)

NAFNet (Chen et al., ECCV 2022) is a state-of-the-art blind image restoration network based on a Nonlinear Activation Free block design with simplified channel attention. We apply the pretrained GoPro motion-deblurring checkpoint zero-shot — no fine-tuning on our SV-PSF data.

The network was trained on RGB images; we repeat the grayscale input across three channels for inference and average the output channels back to grayscale. Inference is performed on overlapping \(256 \times 256\) tiles with a 32-pixel Hann-windowed overlap to handle the \(1008 \times 1008\) input without border artifacts. Despite having no knowledge of the PSF structure or encryption key, NAFNet closes the security gap to \(\approx 0\,\text{dB}\), recovering image quality comparable to the authorized decoder.

Restormer (Adversary, Blind Deep Network)

Restormer (Zamir et al., CVPR 2022) is a hierarchical encoder-decoder Transformer for high-resolution image restoration. Its multi-Dconv Head Transposed Attention (MDTA) and Gated-Dconv Feed-Forward Network (GDFN) enable efficient global context modeling without quadratic self-attention cost.

We apply the pretrained motion-deblurring checkpoint zero-shot, using the same tiled inference strategy as NAFNet (\(256 \times 256\) tiles, 32-pixel Hann-windowed overlap). Like NAFNet, Restormer operates entirely blind — it receives only the encrypted image without any knowledge of the PSF. The results confirm the trend: SOTA blind Transformers eliminate the security gap, motivating adversarial PSF optimization in Phase 2.

Optimization Framework Proposed

Once the differentiable metasurface PSF generator is available, we will run a two-phase adversarial training loop to find a parameter set \(\varphi\) that maximises the security gap end-to-end. The optimization objective is:

\[ \max_{\varphi} \; \mathrm{PSNR}\!\left(D_\mathrm{nb}\!\left(B_\varphi(x),\,\varphi\right),\,x\right) \;-\; \lambda \cdot \mathrm{PSNR}\!\left(D_\mathrm{b}\!\left(B_\varphi(x)\right),\,x\right) \] \[ \text{subject to} \quad \varphi \in \Phi_{\mathrm{realizable}} \]

where \(D_\mathrm{nb}\) is the authorized non-blind decoder (receives \(y\) and \(\varphi\)), \(D_\mathrm{b}\) is the blind adversary (receives \(y\) only), \(B_\varphi\) is the differentiable SV-PSF blur operator, and \(\Phi_{\mathrm{realizable}}\) is the manifold of physically realizable metasurface parameters.

Preliminary Results

We evaluate classical baselines on three standard test images, each resized to \(1008 \times 1008\) pixels (a \(7 \times 144\) pixel patch grid, ensuring each patch is substantially larger than the 71-pixel kernel crop). No training data are required: the non-blind decoder uses the exact PSF analytically. All metrics are computed against the clean original.

Roadmap

Week 1 Classical baselines: smooth SV-PSF forward model (bilinear blending), patch-wise Wiener non-blind decoder, Unsupervised Wiener-Hunt blind adversary. Complete
Week 2–3 Deep baselines evaluated zero-shot: DPIR (non-blind, deep denoiser prior), NAFNet and Restormer (blind adversary). Key finding: SOTA blind networks close the security gap to ≈0 dB, confirming adversarial PSF optimization is necessary. Complete
Month 2 Integrate the differentiable PSF generator (co-worker's model). Build the adversarial optimization loop: Phase 1 trains both decoders independently; Phase 2 updates metasurface parameters \(\varphi\) by ascending the security gap gradient \(\partial\Delta\mathcal{L}/\partial\varphi\). Establish the DIV2K training pipeline. Add LPIPS perceptual loss.
Month 3 Ablation study over adversary strength and PSF parameterization. Identify a fabrication-candidate PSF with measurable security gap against SOTA blind networks. Experimental validation. Paper draft.
